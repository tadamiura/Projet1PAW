Mochi

Mochi is a preparation based on sticky rice, called mochi gome “Sticky rice”, which accompanies many recipes in Japan. On the call dango mochi or simply dango When takes the form of dumplings. The mochi is obtained by kneading cooked sticky rice, which then takes on the appearance of a particularly viscous dough.

Moon cake

The moon cake is a Chinese pastry in the shape of a moon eaten during the mid-autumn festival (or festival of moon). It often contains a salted egg yolk (usually duck). The surface is decorated with relief patterns or auspicious sinograms, or indicating the content to facilitate customer choice. There are indeed different kinds, some of which are typical of a given region. To meet the demand of modern consumers, the choice increases from year to year, and in 2006 includes glazed versions.

Japonese cheescake

Japanese cheesecake (also cotton cheesecake or light cheesecake) is a type of sponge cake originated in Hakata, Japan in 1947.[citation needed] It has a less sweet flavor and fewer calories than standard cheesecake, containing less cheese and sugar. The cake is made with cream cheese, butter, sugar, whipped cream and eggs, and is traditionally made in a bain-marie. Similar to chiffon cake, Japanese cheesecake has a fluffy texture produced by whipping egg white and egg yolk separately. The cake was popularised in the 1990s as the signature dish of Uncle Tetsu's Cheesecake bakery.
